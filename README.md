<h1 align="center">TinyFit Jewelry</h1>

Built by someone who wears a size 3 ring and has a 13.5cm wrist.

[**Live Site**](https://humancronadmin.github.io/tiny-fit-jewelry/) &nbsp;|&nbsp; MIT License

## Why this exists

Every jewelry store, same story.
"Oh I love that ring" — check the size — starts at 5. Every time.

Search "small ring" online and you get kids' jewelry, or brands that call themselves "petite-friendly" but start at size 5. Put a standard bracelet on a 14cm wrist and it slides down to your knuckles. Bangles? They literally fly off if you wave your hand.

Nobody was keeping track of which brands actually make small sizes. So I did it myself. Which brands genuinely carry size 3? Which bracelets won't fall off a 14cm wrist? That's all this is — a database of answers to questions I got tired of asking.

## What's in here

- Size data for 31 brands, verified (smallest ring size + shortest bracelet length)
- Database you can filter by size, price, material, and style
- Japanese brands most people outside Japan have never heard of (Agete, 4℃, Nojess — they go down to US 1.5, and the designs are gorgeous)
- Ring size conversion chart (US / JP / EU / UK)
- Guides for both rings and bracelets
- Works offline (PWA)

## How to use it

1. Measure your finger or wrist
2. Filter the database to your size
3. Go to the brand that fits

## Privacy

Runs in your browser. No account, no tracking cookies, no data collection beyond anonymous analytics.

## Know a brand that carries small sizes?

Add it to `data/brands.json` and send a PR.
