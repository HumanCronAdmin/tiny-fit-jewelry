PIN 9: How to Measure Ring Size at Home
======================================================================
Title: How to Measure Your Ring Size at Home (Accurate to 0.5 Size)
Description: You need: string or paper strip + ruler. Wrap around finger base below knuckle. Mark overlap. Measure in mm. That is your circumference. Size 2 = 41.7mm. Size 3 = 44.2mm. Size 4 = 46.8mm. Pro tips: measure at end of day when fingers are largest. Measure the specific finger. Take 2 readings and average. Cold hands = smaller reading, so warm up first. #ringsizing #howtomeasure #ringsize #petitejewelry #tinyfitjewelry

PIN 10: How to Measure Wrist Size
======================================================================
Title: How to Measure Your Wrist for a Bracelet That Actually Fits
Description: Grab a tape measure or paper strip. Measure just ABOVE the wrist bone (the bony bump on the outside). Pull snug, not tight — you should fit one finger underneath. That number in cm is your wrist size. If it is under 14cm, standard bracelets will not fit you. Add 1-1.5cm for your ideal bracelet length. Write it down and save this pin for your next jewelry shopping trip. #wristmeasure #braceletsize #thinwrist #petitebracelet #tinyfitjewelry

PIN 18: Sizing Tips — Measure at End of Day
======================================================================
Title: Measure Your Ring Size at Night (Your Fingers Change Size Daily)
Description: Your fingers are smallest in the morning and largest at the end of the day. Temperature matters too — cold fingers shrink, warm fingers swell. For the most accurate ring size: measure in the evening, at room temperature, after normal activity. Take 2 measurements on different days and average them. At sizes 2-4, even a half-size difference is less than 1.3mm — precision matters more than ever. #ringsizing #jewelrytips #ringsize #petitejewelry #tinyfitjewelry
URL: https://humancronadmin.github.io/tiny-fit-jewelry/ring-sizer.html
