Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about finding jewelry in petite sizes.

Do ring sizes 2-4 really exist for adults?
Yes. Millions of adult women worldwide wear ring sizes 2-4. Japanese brands like Agete and 4C carry these sizes as standard. It is not a children's size — the industry standard is just too big.
What is the smallest ring size available?
US size 1 (JP size 1). Brands like Agete, 4C, and Nojess in Japan carry rings from this size. In the US, Automic Gold offers custom sizing to any size.
Why are Japanese jewelry brands better for petite women?
Japanese women tend to have smaller average hand sizes, so Japanese brands have always designed for smaller sizes. Many carry rings from US size 1.5, while most Western brands start at size 5. See our Japanese Brands Guide.
How do I measure my ring size at home?
Wrap string around your finger at the base, below the knuckle. Mark the overlap, measure in mm. Size 2 = 41.7mm, Size 3 = 44.2mm, Size 4 = 46.8mm. Measure in the evening at room temperature for best accuracy. Use our Ring Sizer Tool.
What bracelet length do I need for a thin wrist?
Your wrist measurement + 1.5cm = ideal bracelet length. If your wrist is 13cm, look for 14.5cm bracelets. Standard "small" bracelets (15-16cm) are still 2-4cm too big. See our Bracelet Guide.
Is TinyFit Jewelry a store?
No. TinyFit Jewelry is a free size database and guide. We research and verify which brands carry petite sizes, so you can shop directly from those brands with confidence. We may earn a small commission from affiliate links.
How do you verify the size data?
We check each brand's official size charts, contact customer service when needed, and incorporate feedback from real customers. If you want to share your own fit experience, email us and we'll add it.
Can I get a ring resized to size 2-3?
Some jewelers can resize down to size 2-3, but it typically costs $50-150 extra and takes 4-8 weeks. For everyday jewelry, it is more practical to buy from brands that carry your size as standard. See our guide to making rings smaller without resizing.
