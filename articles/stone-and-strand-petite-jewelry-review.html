Stone and Strand: Fine jewelry from US 3, starting at $50

Updated April 16, 2026

Fine jewelry has a price problem for petite buyers. You finally find a brand that makes your size, and the starting price is $300. Then $500. Then you are back to browsing fashion jewelry that chips after a month. Stone and Strand sits in the gap between those two worlds: 14K gold and diamond pieces starting at $50, in sizes that include US 3.

What This Brand Offers

Stone and Strand is a NYC-based brand. Rings start at US size 3. Bracelets start at 15cm with adjustable chain options. Materials are 14K gold and diamond. Prices range from $50 to $800.

Why the Price Floor Matters

At $50, you can buy a 14K gold ring that fits your US 3 finger without treating it as a major financial decision. That changes how you shop. Instead of one ring that you save for special occasions, you can build a collection over time. A stacking ring this month. A band next month. The low entry point turns fine jewelry from a one-time event into an ongoing wardrobe.

What Petite Wearers Notice

The designs run dainty by default. Thin bands, small diamond accents, low-profile settings. On a petite hand, this means the jewelry does not look scaled down — it looks like it was designed at this proportion from the start. The adjustable chain bracelets go below 15cm, which extends the brand's relevance past rings.

Practical Fit Notes

Not every style is available at US 3 — check individual product pages. Fixed-length bracelets at 15cm will be too large for very small wrists; stick to the adjustable chain options. The $50 entry point is for simpler pieces; diamond-heavy designs climb toward $800.

Who This Is For

If fine jewelry has felt out of reach because of price or size, Stone and Strand removes both barriers at once. US 3 rings at $50. Adjustable bracelets. 14K gold. This is where "someday" becomes "today."

Bottom Line

14K gold from $50. Rings from US 3. Dainty by design. Stone and Strand makes fine jewelry reachable for petite buyers who were priced out or sized out of every other option.
