AUrate: Rings from US 3 and bracelets that adjust for small wrists

Updated April 16, 2026

Finding a ring that fits is one problem. Finding a bracelet that fits is another. Finding both from the same brand, in the same order, in the same style — that almost never happens when you are petite. AUrate is one of the few brands where you can shop for both without switching tabs.

What This Brand Offers

AUrate is a NYC-based fine jewelry brand. Rings start at US size 3. Bracelets start at 15cm, with adjustable chain styles that can fit smaller. Materials are 14K gold, 18K gold, and some diamond pieces. Prices range from $50 to $600.

Why Both Categories Matter

When you find a ring brand you love, the bracelet section is often a dead end. The smallest option is 16cm or 17cm, and your 14cm wrist is left out. AUrate's adjustable chain bracelets can cinch below 15cm, which means you are not limited to rings only. Shopping in one place, with one aesthetic, for both your fingers and your wrist — that simplifies a process that is already harder than it should be.

What Petite Wearers Notice

The minimalist designs look proportionate on small hands without appearing oversized. The adjustable bracelets use a sliding mechanism that holds without slipping back. At $50 to $600, the range is wide enough that both an everyday stackable and a special-occasion piece are realistic purchases.

Practical Fit Notes

The bracelet minimum is listed as 15cm, but adjustable chain styles go below that. Fixed-length bracelets will not work for very small wrists. Rings at US 3 are available across most styles, but check individual product pages for confirmation.

Who This Is For

If you want rings and bracelets from the same brand, with a modern minimalist look, and you don't want to spend over $600, AUrate covers both categories. For someone tired of browsing five different brands to dress one hand, this is a relief.

Bottom Line

Rings from US 3. Adjustable bracelets for small wrists. One brand, one aesthetic, both covered. AUrate makes a small hand feel like a complete thought, not a problem split across two shopping lists.
