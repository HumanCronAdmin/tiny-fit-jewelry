enewton: The only bracelet line built for 14cm wrists

Updated April 16, 2026

Most bracelets are not made for you. You know this because every chain bracelet you have tried hangs past your wrist bone, every bangle slides over your hand and onto the table, and every "adjustable" design still has two links too many. At some point you stopped looking.

What This Brand Offers

enewton is a USA brand that makes gold-filled and gemstone bead bracelets. Their eGirl line is designed for 5.5-inch wrists, which is about 14cm. Prices range from $30 to $150. They do not make rings. This is a bracelet brand.

Why the eGirl Line Changes Things

Other brands offer "small" as an afterthought. enewton gave it a name. The eGirl collection is not a regular bracelet made shorter. It was designed from the start for a 14cm wrist. That distinction matters. The bead spacing, the stretch tension, the overall proportion — all of it was set for a smaller circumference. When you put one on, it sits where a bracelet is supposed to sit, not halfway up your forearm.

What Petite Wearers Notice

The stretch construction means you slide it on without a clasp, which removes the awkward one-handed fumble. Petite wearers mention that the beads stay in place throughout the day rather than bunching at the wrist bone. The gold-filled material holds up better than plating over time.

Practical Fit Notes

If your wrist is under 13cm, even the eGirl line may be loose. The stretch design means there is a lower limit to how small it can go. These are not adjustable in the traditional sense — the elastic does the work. For wrists at or near 14cm, the fit is where it should be.

Who This Is For

If you gave up on bracelets because nothing fit your wrist, the eGirl line is worth trying. At $30 to $150, the risk is low. The style is casual — bead stacks, not fine jewelry. If you want gold chains or metal cuffs, look elsewhere. If you want something that stays put on a 14cm wrist without modification, this is it.

Bottom Line

enewton did something rare: they made a product line and said "this is for small wrists" out loud. The eGirl collection fits 14cm wrists by design, not by accident.
