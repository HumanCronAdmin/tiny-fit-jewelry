Soko: When "Adjustable" Means It Fits *From* a US 4 Ring or 15cm Bracelet

Updated April 17, 2026

You know that feeling, don't you? The one where you're constantly having to adjust things that are supposed to fit, but just don't quite make it. Maybe it was a uniform from a first job, where the skirts were always too long, and you spent precious time after work carefully hemming them yourself. It was a hassle, sure, but a hem that dragged or bunched up wasn't just uncomfortable; it could be a safety hazard. Or perhaps you tried to punch extra holes in a belt, only to find the fit was still off, and the whole silhouette looked wrong. It’s that constant, low-level frustration that standard sizing often overlooks smaller frames. And when it comes to jewelry, it's the exact same story. How many "adjustable" bracelets have you seen that still slip right off your 13cm wrist, or rings that spin endlessly on your US 3 finger?

That's why it’s always a good day when you find a brand that's upfront about its sizing, even if it doesn't fit your smallest measurements. Soko, a brand with roots in Kenya and the USA, caught our attention because they clearly state their minimums. For rings, they start at US 4. For bracelets, the minimum is 15cm. This kind of direct information is very helpful, especially when you wear a US 3 ring and have a 13cm wrist. It means you know exactly what to expect, and more , where the limitations are. "Adjustable" doesn't always mean it shrinks down to any size; sometimes, it means it can be fine-tuned *from* its stated starting point.

You won't have to worry about cracking any plating or feeling like you're going to break the piece. This kind of adjustment is all about giving you a secure, comfortable fit once you meet their minimum size requirement.

Your Reality: Rings, Bracelets, and the Petite Fit Gap

Let's talk directly about fit, especially for those of us with smaller measurements. If your ring finger is a US 3, Soko’s rings, which start at US 4, will unfortunately be too large for you. You know that feeling when a ring is just a size too big – it spins constantly, feels loose, and just doesn't look like it belongs on your hand. While Soko does offer adjustable options in some styles, their stated minimum of US 4 is firm. It’s important to respect that boundary; you can't really force a US 4 to fit a US 3 without it being noticeably oversized and uncomfortable.

The same applies to their bracelets. If your wrist measures 13cm, Soko’s bracelets, with a minimum size of 15cm, will also be too big. You’ve likely experienced it before: a bracelet that’s too large will just slide right off, or sit so loosely it’s constantly getting in the way. Even with their adjustable features, the brand clearly states 15cm as the starting point. And , that transparency is something to appreciate. It means you don't have to waste time or money hoping an "adjustable" piece will magically shrink to fit your wrist. If your wrist is 15cm or larger, Soko’s adjustable bracelets are a great option for achieving a tailored, secure fit. But for those with wrists smaller than 15cm, they simply won't work.

It’s a common frustration for anyone with smaller measurements: finding a brand that understands and caters to those sizes, rather than just offering "adjustable" pieces that still start too big. Soko helps cut through some of that guesswork by being clear about their minimums. They offer a distinct style, ethical production, and pieces that carry a sense of warmth and care.
