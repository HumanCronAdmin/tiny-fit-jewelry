You can grab it, use it, wash it, and if you lose it, it’s no big deal. That’s the kind of uncomplicated relationship you want with your jewelry. Something you can put on and forget about, not something that adds another layer of anxiety to your day.

It means these pieces aren't so precious that you’re afraid to wear them out of the house. You can wear them daily without the constant worry of damage or loss. It’s a relief to finally have jewelry that feels like it’s meant to be lived in, not just admired from afar.

When a Ring Size Isn't a Joke

This is where it gets real for those of us with smaller hands. Your US size 3 fingers have been rejected by countless jewelry brands that only start at US size 5 or 6. It’s like they’re not even trying to fit smaller hands, leaving you with rings that constantly spin or threaten to slip off.

Ana Luisa’s smallest ring size is US 4. For someone who wears a US 3, this is a significant step up from the usual. It’s not a perfect fit, no, but it’s a whole lot closer. The rings don’t spin around your finger quite as wildly. It’s the difference between jewelry that feels like an afterthought and jewelry that feels almost right. It’s still a size larger than what you need, but it's a meaningful improvement in an industry that often overlooks us. If you’re a US 4 or larger, you’ll likely find their rings fit comfortably and securely.

Now, let’s talk bracelets. Your wrist is 13cm. Ana Luisa’s minimum bracelet size is 15cm. This means their bracelets will be too large for your wrist. It's important to be direct about this limitation. While 15cm might be better than some even larger "standard" sizes, it still won't fit a 13cm wrist securely. The brand does not offer adjustable options, so there’s no way to make these bracelets work for wrists smaller than 15cm. If your wrist is 15cm or larger, however, you'll find their pieces designed to fit without sliding off.
