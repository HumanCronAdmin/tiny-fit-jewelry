AUrate for petite fingers: rings from US 3 in 14K and 18K gold

Updated April 13, 2026

I remember the first time I saw a US 3 listed on a size dropdown. Not on a vintage shop. Not on Etsy. On a real, established jewelry brand's website. I sat there for a second, just staring at it. AUrate had it right there, between US 2.5 and US 3.5, like it was the most normal thing in the world. And for a moment, it felt like it was.

The actual specs

  • Smallest ring: US size 3
  • Smallest bracelet: 15cm (adjustable styles available)
  • Materials: 14K gold, 18K gold, diamond
  • Price range: $50 -- $600
  • Based in: New York City, USA

What it feels like to shop here

Most fine jewelry brands start at US 5. Some will go to 4 if you ask nicely. AUrate starts at 3. That one number changes the whole experience. You're not emailing customer service asking "do you do custom sizing?" You're not adding a ring to cart knowing you'll need to get it resized. You just pick your size and check out. That's it. The ordinary act of buying a ring, which everyone else has always had, suddenly yours too.

Their rings are 14K or 18K gold. Real gold, not plated, not vermeil. On a small finger, the difference matters more than you'd think. A thin 14K band catches light without looking heavy. It sits flat. It stays put. When the piece is proportional to your hand, you notice the material more, not less.

Bracelets: know the limits

Their bracelets start at 15cm. Some styles have adjustable clasps or multiple jump ring positions that let you shorten the fit slightly. But 15cm is the stated minimum. If your wrist is significantly under that, the bracelet will slide around. Check each product page for whether adjustability is mentioned -- it varies by design.

Who this works for

If your ring size is US 3 or above, AUrate is one of the few fine jewelry brands where you can shop without compromise. The $50 end gets you simple gold pieces. The $600 end gets you diamonds. For bracelets, you need a 15cm wrist or close to it. If your wrist is 13cm, this isn't the one -- the adjustable styles won't close that gap.

But if you've spent years walking past jewelry counters because nothing fits, and you want real gold on your finger without a special order, this is the place where that becomes ordinary. And ordinary, when you've never had it, feels pretty good.
