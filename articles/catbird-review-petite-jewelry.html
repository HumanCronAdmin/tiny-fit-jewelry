Catbird: Delicate Rings for Petite Fingers

Updated April 11, 2026

Most jewelry brands design for an average hand and then scale the size down. The proportions stay the same — the band width, the setting height, the visual weight. On a larger hand, it works. On yours, it looks like someone shrank a ring that was never meant for you. Catbird does something different. Their Threadbare line was designed to be thin, light, and minimal from the start. It is not a regular ring made smaller. It is a ring that looks its best on a slender finger.

What This Brand Offers

Catbird is a Brooklyn-based brand. Materials are 14K gold and silver. Rings start at US size 3. Prices range from $40 to $500. The aesthetic is minimal and fine — pieces that sit close to the skin rather than standing tall above it.

Designed for Your Finger

You have probably tried on a ring that looked beautiful in a photo and then felt wrong on your hand — too wide, too heavy, the proportions off in a way that is hard to explain. That happens when a design was created for a different hand and then offered in your size as an afterthought. Catbird's Threadbare rings were drawn at this scale. The band is barely there. The gold catches light without weighing your hand down. The design is complete when it is on your finger — not before.

What Petite Wearers Notice

The comfort is the first thing. A ring this thin does not press against neighboring fingers or catch on fabric. You forget it is there, which is exactly the point. The second thing is how it looks in the context of your whole hand. A ring that was designed for slender proportions does not compete with your fingers. It follows them.

Practical Fit Notes

Not every Catbird style reaches US 3. The Threadbare line does, and it is their strongest offering for petite fingers. Check individual product pages before purchasing. Bracelets start at 15cm and are not suitable for wrists under 14cm.

Who This Is For

If you want a ring that was designed to look right on a slender finger — not a regular ring that happens to come in your size — Catbird is where to start. The Threadbare line at $40 to $500 makes it realistic to build a collection of rings that were made for the way your hand looks.

Bottom Line

A ring that is complete when it meets your finger. Catbird designs for the proportions you have, not the ones the industry assumes.
