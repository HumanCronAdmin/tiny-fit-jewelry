Why This Site Exists

The short version

I'm 153cm. My ring finger is a size 3. My wrist is 13.5cm.

I got tired of jewelry not fitting. So I made a list of brands that do. That's basically it.

The longer version

Every time I walk into a jewelry store, the same thing happens. I see something beautiful. I check the size. Smallest available: 5. Every. Single. Time.

Online shopping is somehow worse. Search "small ring" and you get children's jewelry covered in butterflies. Search "petite jewelry" and every brand that comes up starts at size 5. How is that petite? In what universe is size 5 petite?

Bracelets are a whole other thing. Standard ones slide down to my knuckles. Bangles fly off my hand if I gesture while talking. I have literally lost jewelry mid-conversation.

For years I kept a mental list of which brands actually carry my size. Which Japanese sites ship internationally. Which Etsy sellers will do custom sizing without charging double. One day it hit me: why isn't there just a list somewhere?

So I made one. No big mission statement. No "empowering petite women" corporate speak. Just a database of brands that make jewelry in sizes that exist on my body.

How we curate

  • Every brand is checked for their actual minimum size — not what they market as "petite"
  • Japanese brands are highlighted because they carry sizes from US 1.5. Most people outside Japan have no idea these exist
  • All data comes from official brand size charts, not assumptions or secondhand info

What this site is NOT

  • Not a store. We don't sell anything.
  • Not sponsored. No brand pays to be listed here.
  • Not ad-free, though. Affiliate links exist and are clearly disclosed. If you buy something through our links, we earn a small commission. It costs you nothing extra and it keeps this site running.

If you're reading this, you probably already know the frustration. I hope this saves you some time.
